Explore Manila's Tourist Spots

Discover iconic landmarks, vibrant culture, and hidden gems waiting for you.

Must-See Tourist Spots

🏰 Intramuros

Explore Manila's historic walled city, rich in Spanish colonial history.

★★★★☆ (4.5)

📝 Local Tip: Visit at sunset for beautiful golden-hour photos and cool breezes!
“Walking through Intramuros feels like stepping back in time. A must-see!”

🌳 Rizal Park

Visit the famous park dedicated to the national hero, Jose Rizal.

★★★★☆ (4.3)

📝 Local Tip: Visit early in the morning to enjoy peaceful walks and catch the flag-raising ceremony.
“A relaxing green space in the heart of Manila. Great for picnics and reflecting on history.” – Liza M.

🐠 Manila Ocean Park

Dive into an aquatic adventure at Manila’s largest oceanarium.

★★★★☆ (4.4)

📝 Local Tip: Weekday mornings are the best time to avoid crowds, especially for the Sea Lion Show.
“My kids loved the jellyfish display and the ocean tunnel – so magical!” – Ron V.

⛪ San Agustin Church

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the oldest churches in the Philippines.

★★★★★ (4.8)

📝 Local Tip: Join a free guided tour for deeper insight into the church's centuries-old history.
“The architecture is breathtaking. It's like stepping into the past.” – Anna C.

🎭 Cultural Center of the Philippines

Home to performing arts, galleries, and Filipino creativity.

★★★★☆ (4.6)

📝 Local Tip: Check their official site for free exhibits or student-discounted shows.
“A haven for art lovers! The shows and galleries are world-class.” – Marc D.

🖼️ National Museum of Fine Arts

Admire masterpieces of Filipino art and culture, including works by Juan Luna.

★★★★☆ (4.7)

📝 Local Tip: Entry is free! Plan your visit around mid-morning to avoid lines.
“Seeing Spoliarium in person gave me chills. Proud of our heritage.” – Bea T.

🍜 Binondo

The world’s oldest Chinatown, offering rich history and amazing Chinese-Filipino food.

★★★★☆ (4.6)

📝 Local Tip: Don’t miss the dumplings at Dong Bei!
“A foodie’s paradise! Every alley has a hidden gem.” – Carla M.

🌸 Paco Park

A peaceful circular park that once served as a cemetery during the Spanish era.

★★★★☆ (4.4)

📝 Local Tip: Go during late afternoon to catch free concerts!
“Quiet, clean, and beautiful—perfect for a slow walk.” – Jess A.

🙏 Quiapo Church

Famous for the Black Nazarene and its deeply rooted Filipino Catholic faith.

★★★★☆ (4.5)

📝 Local Tip: Visit early Friday morning to avoid the crowds.
“Spiritual and intense. You’ll feel the energy of devotion.” – Leah G.

🎡 Star City

A classic amusement park offering thrilling rides and family-friendly fun.

★★★☆☆ (4.2)

📝 Local Tip: Nighttime visits offer cooler weather and vibrant lights!
“Nostalgic fun for all ages. The Ferris wheel at night is a vibe!” – Dave R.

🌿 Arroceros Forest Park

A lush green sanctuary in the city, perfect for nature lovers and quiet walks.

★★★★☆ (4.5)

📝 Local Tip: Bring water and wear comfy shoes for exploring trails!
“Didn’t expect this kind of nature escape right in the city.” – Mia T.

🛍️ SM Mall of Asia (MOA)

One of the largest malls in Asia, offering shopping, dining, seaside views, and family attractions.

★★★★☆ (4.6)

📝 Local Tip: Time your visit around sunset for a relaxing stroll along the baywalk with street performers and snacks.
“So much to do in one place — shopping, skating, rides, and food by the sea!” – Jamie R.
